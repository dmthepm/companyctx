# v0.4 partner-integration re-validation — 100 sites, 30-pair structural envelope re-grade, cache + Google Places measured

> **Scope note on the head-to-head term.** "Head-to-head" in this report
> means the **structural envelope re-grade** over 30 sample rows whose
> hosts have a matching rendered `research-brief.md` in the partner
> repo. The grader reads the v0.4 envelope only; it does **not** read
> the brief contents. PR #90's per-bucket split was a human-in-the-loop
> qualitative read of brief vs envelope — that qualitative pass is
> explicitly out of scope here (see §3 "Grading method" and §6 scope
> boundary). The brief-side check was deferred, so the acceptance box
> for the head-to-head item is marked partially met in the issue
> thread, not fully. The reviews bucket re-grade is fully automatable
> from the envelope and is the load-bearing delta.

## 1. Summary — verdict

**Conditional go — the envelope-level evidence for a partner drop-in
Phase 1 swap is strong; one acceptance check (brief-side qualitative
read) is deferred.** On a 100-site stratified run against
`companyctx==0.4.0` installed from PyPI (not editable main), every
v0.2→v0.4 capability addition delivered measurable movement on the
envelope shape the partner's D100 Phase 1 workflow consumes. The
head-to-head §3 compares envelope shape to *brief existence* (not
brief content) — the reviews bucket is fully re-graded because the
verdict is envelope-derivable; the raw-text-muscle buckets show the
envelope-shape delta but do not resolve the per-brief qualitative
question. See §3's "Grading method" paragraph + §6 scope boundary.

- **FM-7 honesty contract (#79) — works as designed.** 15 / 100 sites
  (15.0 %) came back `status: partial + error.code: empty_response` with
  a `homepage_text` under the 1024-byte floor. Those are sites that
  would have been silent-`ok` on v0.2 — v0.4 correctly tells downstream
  synthesis "don't brief from this envelope." Spot-verified on four of
  the 15: all emit the documented shape (`site_text_trafilatura.status:
  failed, error: empty_response`, `data.pages.homepage_text: ""`,
  reviews provider still populating where available).
- **Google Places reviews (#7) — ships, populates 91 / 100.** `data.reviews.
  rating` + `data.reviews.count` land on **91 / 100** sites — well above
  the 70 % threshold the issue body flagged as "weaker than expected."
  Median rating 4.9 (range 3.6 – 5.0), median count 227, max 7421.
  Per-site cost averages 5.8 ¢; whole-batch spend on Places was **$5.82**
  against the 100-site run. On the 30 head-to-head pairs the Reviews
  bucket moves from **29 / 30 out_of_scope** (v0.2) to **2 / 30
  out_of_scope** (v0.4). That single shift is the biggest v0.2→v0.4
  partner-value delta.
- **Cache (#9) — 100 % hit rate on re-run.** A 20-site re-run against
  the same subset hit cache on **20 / 20** rows. Median subprocess
  wall-clock dropped **5,282 ms → 240 ms** (22× faster). Partner pays
  **$0** in incremental Google Places credits on the re-run (every
  provider short-circuits; the cached envelope preserves the original
  `cost_incurred` as a historical artifact — see §4). On a 20-prospect
  batch re-run, that's ~$1.16 of real spend avoided.

The v0.2 verdict was "not yet partner-ready." The three gaps named
there — #79 empty_response, #7 Google Places, #9 cache — all shipped, and
the measurement shows each one landing where the design intended. The
remaining gap for "full partner-readiness" is **#58** (FM-12 press /
awards / media mentions) — **30 / 30 out_of_scope** on the head-to-head,
unchanged from v0.2. That's the v0.5 target.

**Secondary finding — issue #108 §4 expectation is slightly off.** The
scope body predicted FM-7 sites would return `status: degraded +
error.code: empty_response`. v0.4 actually emits `status: partial`
because the Google Places provider succeeds *in parallel* with the failed
site_text provider, and the composite envelope status reflects that one
of the two providers succeeded. Partial is more informative than
degraded here — the partner's synthesis step still gets reviews data on
a thin-body site — and downstream code can still branch on
`envelope.status != "ok"`. Filed as an editorial follow-up (§7 item A).

| `status: "partial"` rate | 3.3 % | **27.0 %** |

The ok-rate **drop from 96.7 % to 73.0 % is the honesty-contract fix
landing**, not a regression. v0.2 laundered 19.6 % of runs as `ok` with
thin bodies; v0.4 re-labels them honestly (`partial` + `empty_response`)

synthesis that branches on `envelope.status != "ok"` now sees 27 rows it
should skip rather than 7.

FM-7 at the **1024-byte floor measured 15.0 % on v0.4 vs 19.6 % on v0.2
(archived)**. Lower, but the comparison is across different site
distributions — v0.4's sample is a fresh stratified draw across 16
niches (the same-shape-not-same-sites decision per issue #108 scope). On
the overlap niches both measurements exercise (gutter, real-estate-*,
virtual-staging, waste-management) the FM-7 rate is consistent with
#103's post-v0.4 threshold-fit findings (see
`research/2026-04-23-cox-54-post-v0.4-threshold-fit.md` for the narrow
threshold measurement).

## 3. Comparison vs v0.2 baseline — structural envelope re-grade (30 pairs)

**n = 30**, fresh stratified draw from the 64 rows in our 100-site
sample that had a matching rendered `research-brief.md` in the partner
repo's `outputs/*/research-brief.md` (same-shape-not-same-sites per
issue #108 scope; orchestrator decision #3). Proportional distribution
across 12 of the 16 sampled niches.

**Grading method.** Structural classifier (see
`scripts/cox58-grade-head-to-head.py` preamble for the limits): v0.4
envelope's `data.reviews` and `data.pages.*` fields determine the
verdict per bucket. The four "raw-text muscle" buckets
(differentiator, audience, credentials) collapse to the same verdict
because they're all derived from `homepage_text + about_text` — the
v0.2 report's per-bucket human-read split was a qualitative reviewer
judgment this automated re-grade cannot reproduce. The structural
re-grade captures the v0.4 *envelope shape* delta (FM-7 now fires;
reviews now populate); qualitative re-read of the actual brief-vs-
envelope would be a follow-up if the structural verdict says "close but
unclear."

### Per-bucket verdict counts

| Bucket | v0.2 counts (#90 §4, n=30) | v0.4 counts (this report, n=30) |
|---|---|---|
| differentiator | 8 covered_now + 13 covered_via_LLM + 5 partial + 4 bug | **23 covered_via_LLM + 4 bug_thin_body + 3 not_covered** |
| audience | 7 + 17 + 2 + 4 | **23 covered_via_LLM + 4 bug_thin_body + 3 not_covered** |
| credentials | 5 + 14 + 6 + 4 + 1 not_covered | **23 covered_via_LLM + 4 bug_thin_body + 3 not_covered** |
| content_social | 28 out_of_scope + 2 partial | **29 out_of_scope + 1 covered_now** |
| reviews | **29 out_of_scope + 1 covered_via_LLM** | **28 covered_now + 2 out_of_scope** ← flipped |
| media_mentions | 30 out_of_scope | 30 out_of_scope (no change) |

Read in English:

- **Reviews bucket flipped from "out-of-scope on 29 of 30" to "covered
  with hard numbers on 28 of 30."** This is the single biggest partner-
  value delta in the v0.2→v0.4 window. On v0.2, the partner's brief
  sometimes cited a Google rating from search-snippet guesses
  ("approximate"); v0.4's envelope carries the authoritative `data.
  reviews.rating + count` with a `source: reviews_google_places`
  provenance tag.
- **bug_thin_body = 4 / 30 on v0.4.** The 4 / 30 count is a coincidence
  with PR #90's 4 / 30 count — not a per-site comparison. PR #90's
  specific 4 slugs live under `.context/cox-46/` on Devon's machine
  and aren't present in this worktree (see §6 scope boundary, item 3).
  The v0.4 behavior this report does establish is structural: when
  site_text extraction yields fewer than 1024 bytes, the envelope now
  emits `partial + empty_response` rather than silent `ok`. Four fresh
  v0.4 FM-7 slugs were spot-verified for that shape (§2.4). What PR
  #90's specific 4 slugs now do on v0.4 remains unverified and is
  captured as a deferred acceptance item.
- **content_social unchanged** — 29 out_of_scope (v0.2: 28). One
  `covered_now` came from a site whose homepage footer links a
  documented Instagram handle in the extracted `homepage_text`. Follower
  counts remain out-of-scope (no provider ships; by SPEC design).
- **media_mentions still 30 / 30 out_of_scope.** The FM-12 Brave-search
  stub (#58) remains the v0.5 partner-readiness gap.

**Bucket-level "why the raw-text muscle looks flatter in v0.4":** the
structural grader collapses the three raw-text buckets to one verdict
because the envelope shape is identical across them — v0.4 hasn't
invented new columns. PR #90's 13/17/14 covered_via_LLM split by bucket
was a reviewer reading the brief for bucket-specific density; that's
not a v0.2→v0.4 code change to measure. What *did* change is the four
`bug_thin_body` rows now carry honest envelope status and the partner's
agent gets reviews-bucket signal where it used to get nothing. The
grader's apparent "flatness" is an honest measurement of the envelope
delta, not a regression.

## 4. Cost + latency deltas — per-prospect and per-batch

**Per-prospect.** One deterministic `companyctx fetch` on v0.4 with
`GOOGLE_PLACES_API_KEY` set:

- Wall-clock: **~4.7 s p50** subprocess, with ~3.7 s for site_text and
  ~0.6 s for reviews running serially.
- Cost: **6 ¢ median** (Places Text Search 3.2 ¢ + Details
  Basic+Atmosphere 2.2 ¢; 9 / 100 sites return ZERO_RESULTS and bill
  only 4 ¢ Text-Search). **0 ¢** on a cache hit (second run).

**Per-batch (20 prospects, partner workload shape).**

| Dimension | v0.2 baseline | v0.4 first run | v0.4 cache re-run |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wall-clock (sum of subprocess walls) | ~80 s (4 s × 20) | ~95 s (4.7 s × 20) | **~5 s (0.24 s × 20)** |
| Cost — site_text | $0 | $0 | $0 |
| Cost — Google Places | $0 (no provider) | **$1.16** (6 ¢ × 20 avg, less if ZERO_RESULTS) | **$0.00** |
| Partner-readable signals delivered | pages_text only | pages_text + reviews rating + count | pages_text + reviews rating + count |

**Comparison against the partner's already-measured total Phase-1 batch
cost.** PR #90 §6 reported one partner session trailer at
`api_cost_usd = 31.19` across 51 minutes for a 20-prospect batch
(all 3 D100 phases combined). The `companyctx` contribution to that
batch:

- **First-run Places spend: $1.16** on the 20 prospects (Reviews bucket
  coverage).
- **Zero re-run spend** — the cached batch carries partner-actionable
  signal without further API budget.

On a $31 partner batch, `companyctx` v0.4 Places is a **~3.7 % line
item** the first time and **0 %** on re-runs. The deterministic muscle
still doesn't save the partner tokens on the *synthesis* turn (that
cost lives in the LLM layer `companyctx` does not touch) — what it
delivers is the reviews-bucket data the partner was guessing at on v0.2.

## 5. Remaining gaps for full partner-readiness

Two, ordered by partner-workflow impact:

### A. FM-12 press / awards / mentions provider — #58 (COX-28)

`data.mentions` is **30 / 30 out_of_scope** on the head-to-head sample,
unchanged from v0.2. The partner's brief's "Media Mentions" section
relies on a web-search turn the agent runs today; `companyctx` leaves
the slot null with a scaffolded provider (`mentions_brave_stub`). This
is the single remaining bucket that forces the partner's agent to run a
non-`companyctx` turn to close the brief template. **v0.5 primary
candidate** per the issue #108 §5 expected-direction table.

### B. The 12 / 100 `no_provider_succeeded` rows — investigate, don't fix

12 sites failed Attempt 1 with `no_provider_succeeded`; the smart-proxy
provider is not configured on the default zero-key path. In a real
partner deployment the smart-proxy key (`COMPANYCTX_SMART_PROXY_URL`) is
expected to fill in — Attempt 2 recovers the page on anti-bot and
transient-network failures per SPEC §3 waterfall. With no key set here,
those 12 sites fell through and the envelope honestly reports
`no_provider_succeeded`. **Not a v0.4 bug** — a measurement artifact of
running the default zero-key path. Worth noting the envelope shape on
these rows is still useful (partner can skip them), but full partner
deployment will want the Attempt-2 proxy on.

Not a blocker — the honesty contract works the same way either way:
envelope `status != "ok"` on these rows, partner's agent skips
synthesis, agent-actionable suggestion is attached.

### A. Editorial — issue #108 §4 expectation says `status: degraded`; v0.4 emits `status: partial`

The scope body predicted FM-7 sites would return `status: degraded +
error.code: empty_response`. In practice the composite envelope returns
`status: partial` whenever the Google Places provider succeeds in
parallel (which on our sample was 15 / 15 FM-7 sites — reviews
populated on every thin-body site). `partial` is more informative than
`degraded` (reviews bucket still usable) and semantically correct per
the SPEC's status-composition rules (partial = some providers
succeeded). **Proposed: short PR updating the SPEC §28-30 examples +
docs/SCHEMA.md's empty_response example envelope to show the observed
shape rather than the predicted shape.** No code change.

### B. The 9 `ZERO_RESULTS` Places failures — document the 4 ¢ billing floor

9 / 100 sites hit `places textsearch ZERO_RESULTS`, billing only Text
Search (4 ¢) without a Details call. These failures are legitimate (the
business isn't in Google Places or has a hostname that doesn't match a
Places listing) and the provider emits
`places textsearch ZERO_RESULTS: ...`. The `docs/PROVIDERS.md` page
doesn't currently document the 4 ¢ floor on ZERO_RESULTS. **Proposed:
one-sentence docs addition naming the ZERO_RESULTS billing behavior so
agents building cost budgets don't assume 0 ¢ on every failure.** Pure
docs.

### C. Qualitative spot-check of 3-5 head-to-head pairs — tracked as #113

The §3 re-grade is structural (envelope-only; brief paths carried but
not read). The reviewer-flagged gap is that raw-text-muscle bucket
verdicts (`covered_via_LLM`, `bug_thin_body`, etc.) aren't yet
corroborated against human judgment of the rendered brief content.
**#113 picks up the narrow qualitative pass** — 3-5 pairs from the
sample's brief-intersection list, read by hand per PR #90's rubric,
compared against the structural grader's output per-bucket. Agreement
or disagreement there is what either validates the §3 numbers or
narrows the grader's rule.

Note — the original scope proposal in this spot was to stabilize the
grader script itself as a permanent harness (rename
`scripts/cox58-grade-head-to-head.py` → `scripts/run-head-to-head-grade.py`).
That promotion is **blocked on #113** — the structural grader has to
match human judgment before it's worth promoting. #113's acceptance
includes "if disagreement rate is >1/3, open a separate issue
narrowing the grader's rule."
